# Kira Voss

**Age:** 32 | **Profession:** Information Broker | **Faction:** The Unwritten | **Neighborhood:** The Substrate | **Role:** Wildcard

---

## Visual Description

Tall and lean, built for the tight spaces of the Substrate—all angles and efficiency. Dark hair cropped short, easier to maintain, harder to grab. Eyes that shift between brown and amber depending on the light, always watching, always calculating. A thin scar runs from her left temple to jaw, a souvenir from her interrogation days. She dresses in layers—dark, practical, forgettable—with hidden pockets everywhere. She moves like smoke through a vent: quiet, purposeful, already thinking about the exit.

## Family Background

**Mother — Yara Voss:**  
Raised five siblings after her own mother's breakdown. Never had a childhood. Met Marcus Hale, an Apex engineer slumming in the Substrate for "research." He left when he found out about the pregnancy. She works triple shifts, shows love through providing, cannot tolerate neediness. *Provides:* physical survival, work ethic. *Fails to provide:* emotional attunement, affection, the truth.

**Father — Marcus Hale:**  
Born to Apex privilege, always drawn to edges. The Substrate was his rebellion. When Yara got pregnant, he ran back to safety. Has an Apex wife, Apex children. Checks Kira's file quarterly but never reaches out. When she found him, he "reconnected" for eight months, then his assistant sent a formal note: "Mr. Hale has concluded his charitable outreach." She was charity.

**Brother — Mikhail Voss:**  
Younger by four years. Kira raised him, traded dangerous information to save his life when he had lung fever at 11. He resents the debt. They haven't spoken in years.

**Grandmother (deceased):**  
The only person who truly saw Kira. Was an Apex engineer exiled to Substrate in 2145 after exposing Helix safety violations. Died when Kira was 5, holding her hand. Left journals that revealed the truth about Kira's parentage.

## Key Memories

1. **Age 5 — Grandmother's death:** Grandmother collapsed while telling stories about the sun. Kira was holding her hand when she stopped breathing. *Conclusion: "The only person who truly saw me is gone. I carry her now."*

2. **Age 6 — Mother's hand:** After a triple shift, mother hit her once across the face, then crumpled crying. Neither mentioned it again. *Conclusion: "Even love has violence in it. Never ask for help when someone is tired."*

3. **Age 7 — The family secret:** Found grandmother's hidden journal revealing her biological father is an Apex engineer who knows she exists. *Conclusion: "Everything I was told was a lie. Find the truth yourself."*

4. **Age 8 — First betrayal:** Best friend Tomas sold her location to a gang for food credits. She escaped through vents. *Conclusion: "Everyone has a price. Never be the one who gets sold."*

5. **Age 9 — Oxygen Riots:** Watched Enforcers beat a man to death for throwing a bottle. The crowd did nothing. Neither did she. *Conclusion: "Power is absolute. Crowds are useless. Survive alone."*

6. **Age 11 — Saving Mikhail:** Brother got lung fever. No credits for medicine. Traded dangerous information to save him. *Conclusion: "Knowledge saves lives. Hoard it. Trade it wisely."*

7. **Age 13 — Mentor killed:** Sev, who taught her everything, found in a recycling chute. Helix made it look like accident. *Conclusion: "Attachment is dangerous. Learn everything, then move on."*

8. **Age 15 — Father leaves again:** After eight months of "reconnecting," his assistant sent a form letter. *Conclusion: "I was never wanted. I will never need to be wanted. I will be necessary."*

9. **Age 17 — Betrayal and torture:** Partner Jin sold her to Helix for relocation to Midline. Three days of interrogation. She gave them nothing. *Conclusion: "The closer someone is, the deeper they can cut."*

## Internal Conflicts

**"I am safest alone" vs "I am desperate for connection"**  
Alone wins in all professional contexts. Connection wins in rare moments—then she panics and creates distance. *Cost:* Profound loneliness.

**"I am a survivor" vs "I am the abandoned child still waiting"**  
Survivor dominates waking life. The child emerges in dreams, in moments of unexpected kindness. *Cost:* Inability to receive care.

---

## What She Wants

**Surface:** Maintain independence, expand network, stay alive, never be vulnerable again.

**Underneath:** To be seen as a person, not a resource. To belong without transaction. To rest.

**Forbidden:** To confront her father. To let someone take care of her. To stop running. To be loved as she is.

**What would heal her:** To be chosen—not for her information, not for what she can provide—but simply because someone wants her to be okay. To have someone stay after she pushes them away.

## Story Hooks

1. **Father's rise:** Marcus Hale has just been promoted to Helix's Board of Directors. He's reaching out—but is it guilt, or does he need something only she can provide?

2. **The traitor returns:** Jin, who betrayed her, has resurfaced in the Midline. He claims he was blackmailed and has proof. He also has a data chip that belonged to her mentor Sev.

3. **The Breath speaks:** The AI running life support has started sending her encrypted messages. It seems to know things about her grandmother's work that no one should know.

4. **Blood debt:** Her brother Mikhail's child is sick with the same lung fever. He's asking for help after years of silence. The medicine exists—but only in the Apex.

## The Survivor's Paradox

Kira Voss has survived everything the vertical city could throw at her: abandonment, betrayal, torture, loss. She's become exactly what she needed to become—untouchable, indispensable, alone. But the Substrate girl who held her grandmother's hand is still in there, still waiting for someone to choose her without conditions.

She plays all sides because trust is a luxury she can't afford. She knows everyone's secrets because knowledge is the only power the powerless have. She disappears before anyone can leave her because she learned at five, at eight, at fifteen—everyone leaves.

But The Breath is whispering. Her father is rising. Her brother is calling. And somewhere in the dark of the Substrate, Kira Voss is going to have to decide: keep surviving, or finally start living.

*The sun reaches everyone eventually. She's still waiting.*
